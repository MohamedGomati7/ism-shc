  • General Admission Policy

    1. Parents and guardians who desire an English immersion educational setting for their children.
    2. Parents and guardians and children agree to
    a) Speak and communicate only in English while on School campus.
    b) Cooperate and work together to uphold the rules and regulations of the School.
    3. Parents and Guardians must agree to bring their child to school on time and pick up their child within 15 minutes of the end of the school day.
    4. Parents and Guardians should be aware that ISM School does not have a Special Educational Needs facility. Therefore, we cannot accommodate students with known cases of: ADHD, Dyslexia and any other physical/intellectual/behavioural disorders which are not within our scope to accept. These decisions are entirely at the discretion and decision of the Principal.
    5. Successful performance in School Entrance Exams.
    6. The majority of new students start at the beginning of Term 1 (usually September), however we can also accept students during the Academic Year at the discretion of the Principal. These mid-year acceptances are dependent on
    a) students meeting all admissions requirements,
    b)availability of spaces and
    c) assessment on whether or not the student can reasonably satisfy the demands of the course.

  • Priority of Admission

    1. Students from diplomatic and foreign service families stationed in Libya.
    2. Students from overseas and foreign company families posted in Libya.
    3. Students who come from an English immersion household or former overseas residents.
    4. Students who wish to apply for any REMAINING or AVAILABLE spaces, subject to entrance examination, school acceptance and immediate payment of full school fees to secure a place.

  • Pre-Kindergarten & Kindergarten Admissions

    - Children must be 4 years of age by 2nd September to enter our Pre-K program.
    - Children must be 5 years old by 2nd September to enter our Kindergarten program.
    - Note: NO exception will be made to our admission policy on this point.
    - All children entering ISM must be fully toilet-trained, we do not offer creche or nursery facilities. Children who are not capable of handling their own hygiene needs at the time of school entry will unfortunately be asked to move schools.
    - Fees must be paid and registration forms completed and submitted to the Academic Office before the child will be admitted to the Pre-K or Kindergarten program.
    - ISM International School Is not equipped to deal with students with severe physical disabilities. Any exceptions are entirely at the discretion of the Principal.
    -Parents may sit with their child during the first day of school only.
    -After the first day, parents must drop students at the classroom and remove themselves from the school grounds.
    Parents are encouraged to take children to a nursery or a babysitter prior to enrollment in the school.
    This will help with the discomfort and stress of separation between parents and child.
    Also, please remember that children will pick up on your anxieties and tend to become more distressed if their parents are upset.
    Please remain calm for your child.

  • Grade 1 – 6 Admissions Requirements

    - Students must meet age requirements
    - Age on or before the 2nd of September
    Grade 1 – Age 6
    Grade 2 – Age 7
    Grade 3 – Age 8
    Grade 4 – Age 9
    Grade 5 - Age 10
    Grade 6 – Age 11
    - Students will meet with a grade-level teacher who will assess the students suitability for the applied grade. Students will need to be able to communicate with the teacher fluently and easily in English.
    - Where assessment results are ambivalent the student may be enrolled for a probationary period.
    During this period the teachers will supervise the student and evaluate their academic progress.
    Parents have a strong responsibility to assist students and teachers with closing any knowledge gaps.
    - If the student fails their probationary period and does not meet the required standard of English proficiency, they will either be asked to move to the next grade down or leave the school.

  • Grade 7 to 12 Admissions Requirements

    Grade 12
    - No students will be admitted into Grade 12 unless they have studied for the previous 2 years in Ireland.
    Grade 7 to 11
    - Entrance Examinations will be administered in English, Mathematics and Art (Grade 11 only if required).
    - The minimum pass mark to be considered for acceptance to the school is 50% .
    - If the student does not demonstrate a satisfactory proficiency in English at the time of application, the Principal may refuse admission if there is no appropriate syllabus, class or programs to suit the needs of the student.
    - Students may be admitted with a lower score than 50% in one subject ONLY for a probationary period.
    The probation period will end at the end of Term 1.
    If the student does not pass all subjects with a minimum of 50% at this time, the student may be asked to return to the previous grade or asked to leave the school.
    - It will be the parents responsibility to provide extra assistance or tuition to the student during this probationary period AND to make sure their child has no disciplinary conflicts during this time.

I.S.M

ISM International School first opened in 1954 as the Oil Companies School, a school for the children of oil company executives and diplomats posted to Libya. ISM is the oldest international school in North Africa and has successfully graduated every class since 1960.
